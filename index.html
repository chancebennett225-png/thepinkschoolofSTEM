Innovating Science with AI-Powered Learning.

The Pink School of STEM is a non-traditional program designed to cultivate digital literacy, AI proficiency, and a deep understanding of Louisiana Science Standards for middle school girls. We prepare our students to become confident and creative problem-solvers by merging AI with the field of STEM.

About The Pink School of STEM

Our program is an innovative, non-traditional curriculum constructed by skilled STEM educators. We merge AI Literacy with fundamental Biology concepts to elevate the learning curve for middle school girls.

Our Core Principles

Curriculum-Informed AI (CI-AI)

We ensure all AI tools are instructionally aware and aligned with Louisiana Science Standards, embedded within proven teaching strategies.

Interdisciplinary Reasoning

Activities are designed to encourage students to transfer concepts from AI to explain phenomena in science, fostering deep understanding.

Project-Based Learning (PBL)

Our thematic framework avoids expository teaching, emphasizing active learning through real-world problem-solving.

AI Ethics & Digital Literacy

Students learn about bias detection, algorithmic awareness, and responsible use of AI, gaining more than just technical skills.

Our Integrated STEM Curriculum

Our narrative-driven curriculum transforms learning into a mission. Explore the unique challenges and AI integrations for each grade level.

6th Grade: The Structure of Matter

This module explores the fundamental building blocks of the universe. Students investigate the structure of matter and the properties of waves, analyzing them as patterns in physical science.

Science Focus

Structure & Properties of Matter (atoms, molecules); Wave Properties (refraction, reflection).

The Challenge

Analyze patterns of cosmic matter and communication waves to identify the "source code" of new life forms.

AI Integration

Use Google's Teachable Machine to train image classification models, learning about Machine Learning, data input, and algorithmic bias.

7th Grade: The Flow of Energy & Matter

Students track the flow of energy and matter through complex systems. This module connects life science concepts like photosynthesis with Earth science phenomena like atmospheric circulation.

Science Focus

Cycling of Matter & Flow of Energy (Ecosystems); Atmospheric Circulation & Climate.

The Challenge

Predict how disturbances in an ecosystem's energy flow, like in Louisiana wetlands, are recorded by atmospheric patterns.

AI Integration

Develop computational models and use virtual labs. Practice prompt engineering with Generative AI chatbots as personalized tutors.

8th Grade: Engineering & Environmental Solutions

The final mission is to apply problem-solving skills to real-world environmental challenges. Students engage in the engineering design process to create solutions for issues facing Louisiana.

Science Focus

Engineering Design (ETS1); Natural Hazards & Human Impact (Louisiana fossil fuels/coastal issues).

The Challenge

Design a sustainable, AI-monitored system to mitigate human impact on Louisiana's resources or coastal environment.

AI Integration

Use AI to analyze large environmental data sets. Use Generative AI to design and critique solutions, evaluating the validity and reliability of AI-generated claims.
